The Best Movies according to Paulami

My top 3 movies of all time

3 idiots

Directed by Rajkumar Hirani, 3 Idiots is a comedy-drama that addresses the Indian education system's pressures. The story follows three engineering students—Rancho (Aamir Khan), Farhan (R. Madhavan), and Raju (Sharman Joshi)—at a prestigious institution. The film critiques the rote learning approach and societal expectations placed on students. Rancho, an unconventional thinker, inspires his friends to follow their true passions rather than societal expectations. The movie's themes, humor, and emotional depth resonated with audiences, making it a massive success.

Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge

Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge, often abbreviated as DDLJ, is a romantic drama directed by Aditya Chopra. The film stars Shah Rukh Khan as Raj and Kajol as Simran, two young Indians living in London. They meet during a European trip and, despite initial misunderstandings, fall in love. However, Simran's conservative father has already promised her hand in marriage to his friend's son in India. The film explores Raj's efforts to win over Simran's family and convince them of their love. It's a beloved classic known for its iconic dialogues, memorable music, and the chemistry between the lead actors.

Gully Boy

Gully Boy, directed by Zoya Akhtar, is a musical drama inspired by the lives of Mumbai street rappers Divine and Naezy. The film stars Ranveer Singh as Murad, a young man from a poor neighborhood who dreams of becoming a rapper. Alia Bhatt plays Safeena, his supportive but fiery girlfriend. The movie portrays Murad's struggles against societal and familial expectations, and his journey towards self-expression and success through music. Gully Boy was praised for its music, performances, and authentic portrayal of Mumbai's underground rap scene, earning it critical and commercial acclaim.
